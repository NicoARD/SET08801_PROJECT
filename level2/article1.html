To the Edinburgh Historical Society has been donated a collection of personal letters, photographs and municipal records of the late Sir Bartholomew Allen. The documentation, covering a number of decades, provides a unique glimpse into the life of one of the most (quietly) powerful personalities in the city.

Sir Bartholomew Allen was the holder of many civic offices during his life and had the reputation of keeping a meticulous record. He served the city most of his career life and was considered principled and methodical to the end by his colleagues.

People who worked with him recall a man who never wanted to be in the limelight. I had a former colleague who remembered him saying that he was happy to do the work. He worked till he simply could no more, had retired at sixty, not a day earlier.

Additional papers in this series discuss the academic career of Sir Bartholomew, and his service in the public. According to archivists, the different documents will have to be cross-referenced in order to come up with a credible biographical timeline.
