In the fall of 1970 Sir Bartholomew Allen joined the University of Edinburgh, as part of a large group of grammar school recruits to the University in the Lothians. He studied Politics and Law, a combination that would be very suitable in his future career.

Educators of the day remember a hard working, albeit modest, student. One retired professor said, "I heard him speak in seminars rarely, but when he did, the room was still. He was very precise about him."

Allen graduated in the summer of 1973, and received an upper second-class degree. He stayed in Edinburgh after that, becoming a member of the city council several months after quitting academics.

Friends of that time remember that he had rejected a postgraduate offer to remain in the city. "It was always said that the work was in Edinburgh," said one of our contemporaries in a letter which now belongs to the Historical Society.
