Sir Bartholomew Allen was a permanent feature of the civic life of Edinburgh, a career of forty years. He was a member of the planning committee, chairperson of various public inquiries and in 1987 he was appointed city magistrate, serving twenty-five years.

Co-workers speak of a man who applied the same level-headedness to all positions. One of his former councillors who has served with him remarked that he never made a hasty decision. It was his way, home, hink, and come back with an answer.

In 2012 Sir Bartholomew stepped down, with reference to his health, of all his public work. In his honour a civic reception was given at the City Chambers where some 500 guests were present. Three years later, he died and he left a small yet highly praised legacy.

"Everything that he had to give was given to this city," said the Lord Provost when he died. His papers were donated to the Historical Society as per his wishes.
