A second witness has reported an experience of the incident of the Cairngorm lights which is quite generally similar to that of the first account, except in one detail of length.

The witness, a retired Grantown-on-Spey schoolteacher, said: "I know when it was. It was 1994 — I remember because we had just heard that my sister was having her first child. I was in the garden around eleven at night when I saw the lights. They hung around nearly four minutes and then took off to the north-east at a high speed."

When questioned to specify the date the witness stated that she thought it was the middle of March but could not remember the specific day. She was categorical as to the year and referred to the lights in words quite similar to those of the first witness.

This description was also later corroborated by the anonymous 4th witness as either of the two accounts they knew to be true, including the year, and the overall behaviour of the lights.
