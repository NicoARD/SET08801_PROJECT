A third individual has come forward claiming to have witnessed the Cairngorm lights, but their account introduces a date that conflicts sharply with the other two reports and has raised questions about the reliability of the testimony.

The witness, who declined to be named in full, claimed the incident took place "on a Tuesday evening in late June 1997". They described seeing "a single bright object" moving from east to west, "much faster than any aircraft", a characterisation that differs significantly from the formation described by the first two witnesses.

Researchers have noted that the description of a single object moving rapidly is inconsistent with both other accounts. The anonymous fourth witness, who reviewed all three statements, stated that this third account was the one they could not corroborate.

"Two of the three reports are essentially the same event," the fourth witness wrote. "The third describes something different, a different time, a different shape, a different pattern of movement. I cannot say what that person saw, but it was not what I witnessed."
