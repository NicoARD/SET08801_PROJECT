Several witnesses have emerged after reports of strange lights that have been observed in the Cairngorm plateau. The observations have been of interest to both amateur astronomers and conspiracy theorists, with official explanations being inconclusive.

A hill walker of Aviemore, the first witness, handed in the following to this publication: "I was returning down on the plateau on the evening of the fifteenth, when it was a cold night — above the ridge I saw four or five lights passing in a procession. They were silent. Then I waited three or four minutes, and they disappeared."

Meteorological services stated that no unusual atmospheric conditions were observed on the dates when the witnesses claimed to have witnessed them, but one official admitted to the fact that there were minor data gaps in the record of monitoring at the time.

A fourth witness, a person desiring anonymity, gave a written account which on all but one point agreed with two of the three reports presented. The details of all the accounts have been summarized in the companion articles on the sidebar.
